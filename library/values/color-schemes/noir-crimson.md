## Color Roles

- **Primary** (`#F0EDE8`): Warm near-white — HSL(30°, 27%, 93%). All text and core content on the dark canvas. Warm, not clinical — avoids the cold blue-white of pure digital white.
- **Secondary** (`#2A2828`): Deep warm charcoal — supporting surfaces, containers, structural elements lifted slightly off the near-black canvas.
- **Tertiary** (`#DB0037`): Vivid crimson — HSL(345°, 100%, 43%). The CTA, the single vivid signal. Sits at high chroma against the dark ground for maximum chromatic contrast.
- **Neutral** (`#0A0A0A`): Near-absolute black — the canvas. The noir ground everything else rests on.

## Nudge Note

Original crimson `#CC0033` sat at HSL(345°, 100%, 40%) — right at the L floor. Nudged to `#DB0037` at L43% for reliable Tertiary token landing while preserving the deep crimson character.

## Notes

The near-black canvas makes crimson read as a hot signal — dangerous, urgent, precise. Keep it to one element per panel. Warm Primary prevents the design from reading as a cold tech interface.
