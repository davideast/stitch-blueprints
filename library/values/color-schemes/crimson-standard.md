## Color Roles

- **Primary** (`#0A0A0A`): Near-absolute black — HSL(0°, 0%, 4%). All text, headings, core content. Maximum luminance contrast against the light canvas.
- **Secondary** (`#4A4747`): Warm dark gray — supporting structure, captions, metadata, borders. Bridges the near-black Primary and the cream canvas without warmth or chromatic noise.
- **Tertiary** (`#DB0037`): Vivid crimson — HSL(345°, 100%, 43%). The single vivid signal: CTAs, active states, the masthead accent. High chromatic contrast against both the near-black and the cream.
- **Neutral** (`#F7F5F2`): Warm institutional white — HSL(30°, 25%, 97%). The canvas. Slightly warmer than pure white — references archival paper, not a glowing screen.

## Notes

The starkest of the crimson schemes. Near-black type on near-white canvas with one vivid red accent is the foundational grammar of Swiss institutional design. The crimson lands with maximum impact because everything else is achromatic. Restraint in Secondary (warm gray rather than anything vivid) is critical — any chromatic noise in the supporting layer dilutes the crimson's signal.
