A dark-mode editorial experience inspired by antique maps and modern data journalism. Every screen feels like a page from a prestige geographical publication — dense with meaning, sparse with decoration. The interface feels like it was made to be printed and preserved.

## Preset-Specific Rules

These rules are specific to this palette and aesthetic — they do not exist as universal library blueprints.

- **Gold exactly once per panel.** The Tertiary gold is a laser pointer, not a palette. One CTA, one accent element, one coordinate marker — never more per panel composition.
- **No warm backgrounds.** All warmth in the design lives exclusively in the gold accent. The canvas, panels, and surfaces remain entirely cold navy-black.
- **Maximum two type sizes per panel.** Editorial restraint. More than two sizes creates hierarchy noise that competes with the single gold accent.
- **Full-bleed imagery and typography share equal weight.** This is an editorial publication, not a product page. Neither image nor type dominates — they are compositional equals.
