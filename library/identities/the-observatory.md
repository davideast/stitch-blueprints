An astronomical dark-mode identity built on the principle of infinite depth. Choose this when you want the interface to feel like looking through a telescope — cool, precise, and layered in altitude. Every scroll position reveals a distinct atmospheric plane.

## Preset-Specific Rules

These rules are specific to this aesthetic — they do not exist as universal library blueprints.

- **Every scroll depth is a distinct atmospheric layer.** Background (stars, nebulae, gradients) at low opacity and high blur — they recede. Mid-layer (structural frames, grid lines) at medium contrast. Foreground (text, instruments, CTAs) at full contrast and sharpness.
- **Background layers stay low-opacity and blurred.** They suggest depth — they do not compete with foreground content. If a background element is readable, it is too prominent.
- **No warm colors anywhere.** This world is entirely cool and astronomical. Any warm hue — even a neutral warm gray — breaks the palette's logic.
- **No decorative illustration.** Depth comes from real atmospheric layering, not ornament. Every visual element must be functional or atmospheric — never decorative.
