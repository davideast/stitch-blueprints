A massive central container shaped as an arch or tombstone — flat top rounded into a dome using equal border-top-left-radius and border-top-right-radius set to 50%. The arch sits centered on a plain canvas with extreme negative space on all sides. It functions as a portal or window: the content inside the arch is a different world from the canvas outside it.

**The overlapping geometric element:** A perfect circle (or partial arc) positioned absolutely on the boundary line of the arch, overlapping both the arch and the canvas simultaneously. This creates a 2.5D layering effect — the circle appears to exist between the arch and the canvas, not on top of either. A hard-edged offset drop-shadow in a contrasting accent color (rather than a standard blurred shadow) reinforces the physical, printed-object depth.

**The stippled base:** The bottom 10–20% of the arch uses a stippled gradient or halftone dot pattern to dissolve the shape into the canvas rather than ending with a hard edge. Replicates the ink-dot gradient of screen printing.

Use when the design intent is monumental, analog, or esoteric — a single object that the entire page is organized around.
