prompt: >-
  On page load, a full-height hero header or expanded navigation bar occupies the top of the viewport. As the user scrolls past a threshold, the header collapses into a compact sticky bar (48–60px) that follows the user down the page. The transition is smooth — elements scale down, secondary content fades, branding may transform. The compact sticky bar provides navigation and quick actions without obstructing content.
---

A progressive disclosure pattern that rewards scroll — the header presents a rich first impression, then gets out of the way. Choose this for marketing pages, product pages, or editorial content where the above-fold experience is premium but continued reading needs clear navigation. Works particularly well with hero-full-screen or magazine-spread as the primary content layout. Pairs with expressive typography in the expanded state and compact utility typography in the collapsed state.
