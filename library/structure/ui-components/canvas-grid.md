Decorative border grid applied between page sections. Horizontal lines extend the full width of the viewport (edge to edge). Vertical lines remain within the page container width. Together they form a structural grid framework that gives the page a sophisticated, architected feel without requiring custom graphics or illustrations.

Inspired by Stripe, Adio, and Tailwind. Most effective as a finishing layer applied after the core content sections are established. The grid should be low-contrast — a subtle structure, not a competing visual element. Works especially well with sharp (0px) roundedness and editorial layouts where the geometry of the page is itself a design statement.
