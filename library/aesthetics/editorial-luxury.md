Curated restraint. Photography-forward. Every element earns its place.

The interface feels like a physical artifact — something worth holding. Generous whitespace is not emptiness, it is editorial judgment: what was left out matters as much as what is in. Text is sparse and confident. Images breathe. The design communicates that someone with taste made deliberate decisions about every centimeter.

Editorial luxury differs from editorial: where editorial is dense and authoritative, editorial luxury is spare and sensory. The journalism tradition gives way to the gallery tradition — fewer words, larger images, more silence.

Editorial luxury rejects: information density, busy grids, small imagery, utility-first layout, decorative typography that competes with photography.
