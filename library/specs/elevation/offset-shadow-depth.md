Depth is conveyed through hard-edged offset shadows rather than blurred drop shadows. A shadow displaced exactly 4–8px horizontally and vertically with zero blur radius — the element appears lifted off a physical surface, like a die-cut piece of cardstock sitting on a table. The shadow color is a distinct accent (not black or gray) to add esoteric or chromatic interest.

No ambient shadow, no spread. The offset is the only signal. Combined with a flat background canvas (no gradients, no textures beyond grain), the hard shadow creates the most contrast between a "digital" and "analog print" reading.

Used on interactive elements (buttons, cards) that need to feel tangible and pressable, not rendered. On hover, the shadow shifts slightly — the element appears to depress toward the surface, completing the physical metaphor.

Pairs naturally with flat, matte color fills and sharp or zero roundedness. Avoid combining with blurred shadows or glassmorphism — these aesthetics are mutually exclusive.
