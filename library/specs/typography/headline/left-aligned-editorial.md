Three headline conventions that together move a page from AI-default to professionally designed:

**1. Left-align the hero — always**
Heroes are left-aligned, never centered. Split layout: headline occupies roughly 3/5 of the width on the left; subheading and description occupy roughly 2/5 on the right, both top-aligned. This is how Tailwind Plus, Linear, Stripe, and Apple structure heroes. Centered heroes are the single most recognizable AI-generated pattern.

**2. Tight tracking at large sizes**
Any headline above 24px gets `tracking-tight` or tighter. Default letter-spacing at large sizes looks loose and weak — tightening it makes the headline feel compact, intentional, and impactful.

**3. Inline section headings**
Section headings and their subheadings sit on the same line — no line break between them. Heading: dark bold (neutral-950). Subheading: follows immediately in gray medium weight (neutral-600 medium). Works best with long subheading text. Eliminates the standard AI "big heading, break, gray subtitle below" pattern.
